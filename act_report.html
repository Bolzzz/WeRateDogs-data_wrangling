Data Analysis Report: @dog_rates (WeRateDogs)

After carrying out the wrangling steps on the data gathered for @dog_rates, we were tasked with sharing some insights and visualizations.

Question 1: What stage of dogs is the most prominent?

This was geared towards finding the most common age at which a dog is showcased on the account. It is important to note that of the 2356 dog tweets; we could only figure out, and carry out our analysis on, the stage of 321 dogs.

Question 2: What’s the most recurring rating given to the dogs?

Does the account handler rate randomly, or are they very intentional about the ratings of the featured dogs?

Well, we can tell by running a summary statistic on the rating column:

A few things to note:

  • Dogs without images were dropped
  • Rows within the text column that contained retweets were also dropped
  • The rating column was generated by dividing the rating numerator by the rating denominator to get a floating figure (which, if you did not know, really means that it is a continuous figure)

Question 3: From what device/access point are the tweets by @We Rate Dogs mostly published?

The source column was analyzed to find out the most means by which WeRateDogs posted their tweets.

At both points, i.e., before some entries were dropped and after, we see that the Twitter app for iPhone was mostly used in posting (94% and 98% respectively)
